MNT practice questions (Dr. Lam CV57) + selected questions from your CV Pharm Part 2 bank. Attempt before revealing.

MNT Q1. 64M with MI, LVEF 35%, dilated chamber with ↑ wall thickness. ACEi started. Which physiological effect best explains reduced cardiac remodeling?
A. Decreased degradation of bradykinin
B. Direct antagonism of AT1 receptor
C. Increased excretion of K+ and H+
D. Vasoconstriction of efferent arteriole
E. Reflex activation of SNS
Answer: A — Decreased degradation of bradykinin. ACEi inhibit ACE (kininase II), which normally degrades bradykinin. ↑ bradykinin + ↓ tissue AngII together reduce vascular smooth muscle and myocyte growth → anti-remodeling. B is the ARB mechanism. D is wrong direction (ACEi dilates efferent, lowering not raising pressure).
MNT Q2. 58F with HFrEF (EF 30%) on lisinopril. Transitioning to sacubitril/valsartan (ARNI). What is the most appropriate next step?
A. Start ARNI immediately at next dose
B. Discontinue lisinopril and wait 36 hours before starting ARNI
C. Add ARNI to current lisinopril regimen
D. Halve lisinopril dose for one week then switch
E. Replace lisinopril with ARB for two weeks prior
Answer: B — Discontinue lisinopril and wait 36 hours. ARNI contains sacubitril (neprilysin inhibitor) which ↑ bradykinin. ACEi also ↑ bradykinin. Combining them = "double hit" on bradykinin → dangerously high bradykinin → severe angioedema. Must clear ACEi for 36 hours first.
MNT Q3. 72M on metoprolol succinate, lisinopril, and spironolactone. K+ = 5.6, creatinine = 2.1 (baseline 1.1). He has bilateral breast enlargement and tenderness. Cause?
A. Metoprolol succinate
B. Lisinopril
C. Spironolactone
D. Eplerenone
Answer: C — Spironolactone. Non-selective mineralocorticoid antagonist that also blocks androgen and progesterone receptors → gynecomastia and breast tenderness in men. Eplerenone (D) is more selective for mineralocorticoid receptor → does NOT cause gynecomastia. Note also: K+ 5.6 and ↑ creatinine suggest hyperkalemia + AKI from the combination of lisinopril + spironolactone.
MNT Q4. Patient with HFrEF started on carvedilol. Two weeks later: severe fatigue, ↑ peripheral edema, HR 58, JVD. Most likely reason?
A. Direct stimulation of α1 receptors causing vasoconstriction
B. Use of β1-selective agent in patient with lung disease
C. Initiation of beta-blocker before achieving adequate volume stabilization
D. Antagonism of RAAS pathway → Na+ retention
E. Rapid up-titration at 1-week intervals
Answer: C — Started BB before achieving adequate volume stabilization. In decompensated/fluid-overloaded HF, BBs ↓ CO → worsens congestion (JVD, edema). Should have optimized diuresis FIRST. Note: E is also wrong because up-titration should be no less than every 2 weeks (1 week would be too rapid, but the stem says "two weeks later" suggesting a single dose initiation issue, not rapid titration).
Bank Q27. 80M with CHF, EF <30%, worsening dyspnea/fatigue. Physician adds a medication that has NOT been shown to prolong life but will reduce his symptoms. Which medication?
A. Metoprolol   B. Digoxin   C. Empagliflozin   D. Eplerenone   E. Sacubitril/valsartan
Answer: B — Digoxin. Key distinction: Digoxin reduces symptoms and hospitalization but has NOT been shown to reduce mortality in HF. All other options (metoprolol, empagliflozin, eplerenone, sacubitril/valsartan) have proven mortality benefit in HFrEF.
Bank Q28. Which of the following drugs improves survival in chronic systolic HF? (1=Enalapril, 2=Digoxin, 3=Carvedilol, 4=Furosemide)
A. 1,2,3 only   B. 1,3 only   C. 2,4 only   D. 4 only   E. All
Answer: B — 1 and 3 only (Enalapril and Carvedilol). ACEi (enalapril) and beta blockers (carvedilol) = proven mortality benefit. Digoxin (2) = symptom/hospitalization benefit ONLY, no mortality benefit. Furosemide (4) = symptom/congestion relief only, no mortality benefit.
Bank Q30. Which drugs produce an anti-remodeling effect on the failing heart? (1=Spironolactone, 2=Carvedilol, 3=Lisinopril, 4=Furosemide)
A. 1,2,3 only   B. 1,3 only   C. 2,4 only   D. 4 only   E. All
Answer: A — 1, 2, and 3 (Spironolactone, Carvedilol, Lisinopril). MRA, beta blockers, and ACEi/ARNI all have proven anti-remodeling effects. Furosemide (4) is a loop diuretic — reduces congestion/symptoms only, NO anti-remodeling effect.
