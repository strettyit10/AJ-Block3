  • Free edges are typically spared.
  • Classic severe symptom triad: angina, syncope, heart failure.

Rheumatic Heart Disease4 subtopics
HY
  • Immune-mediated sequela of group A strep pharyngitis.
  • Mitral valve is always involved in this lecture framing.
Post-Group A Strep Pharyngitis
HY
  • Acute rheumatic fever classically follows untreated GAS pharyngitis after 2–3 weeks.
Aschoff Bodies
HY
  • Signature lesion of acute rheumatic carditis.
  • Associated with Anitschkow cells.
Mitral Leaflet Thickening
HY
  • Chronic rheumatic disease causes leaflet thickening, commissural fusion, and thickened/fused chordae.
Fish-mouth Stenosis
HY
  • Classic gross morphology of chronic rheumatic mitral stenosis.
  • Can lead to pulmonary hypertension and RV hypertrophy.

Endocarditis3 subtopics
HY
  • Vegetations may be infectious or noninfectious; embolization is a major complication.
Infectious (IE)4 subtopics
HY
  • Vegetations contain organisms plus thrombotic/necrotic debris.
  • Can cause valve destruction, abscesses, and septic emboli.
Acute (S. aureus, Virulent)
HY
  • Often affects previously normal valves and is rapidly destructive.
  • Tricuspid valve is classic in IV drug use.
Subacute (S. viridans, Damaged valves)
HY
  • More indolent course, often on previously abnormal valves, commonly linked to oral flora.
Bulky/Destructive Vegetations
HY
  • Favours acute infective endocarditis rather than NBTE or Libman-Sacks.
Duke Criteria Diagnosis
HY
  • Think positive blood cultures, echo evidence of vegetation/abscess, and new regurgitation.
Non-Bacterial (NBTE)3 subtopics
HY
  • Sterile bland vegetations made of platelets and fibrin.
  • Usually along lines of closure and loosely attached.
Marantic Endocarditis
HY
  • Synonym for NBTE.
Sterile/Reddish Vegetations
HY
  • Main danger is embolization rather than valve destruction.
Hypercoagulable states (Cancer)

  • Classically associated with mucinous adenocarcinoma and other hypercoagulable states.

  • Sterile endocarditis associated with SLE.

HY
  • Autoimmune setting should push you toward Libman-Sacks rather than infectious IE.
